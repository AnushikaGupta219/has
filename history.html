HISTORY

Innovations have always influenced the values of a society and raised new questions in the ethics of technology. we are here for you to provide some ideas and knowledge about the technology in.


• We deal with two worlds in IoT. One is a physical world and other is an information world(virtual). The objects which use this physical world and information world are called “Things”. In other words, any device that can be embedded with electronics, software, sensors to communicate with other device is “Things”. Things are capable of being identified and integrated into communication medium. Physical things are objects in the surrounding environment such as sensors, electrical equipment, electronic devices, etc. Virtual things are capable of being stored, processed and accessed like multimedia content, Facebook, twitter, servers, cloud etc. A Thing, in the Internet of Things, can be a person with a heart monitor implant, a farm animal with a biochip transponder, an automobile that has built-in sensors to alert the driver when tire pressure is low -- or any other natural or man-made object that can be assigned an IP address and provided with the ability to transfer data over a network.


Why IoT?

• Assume that a lady wearing a high-tech spectacle walks into her kitchen. The moment she opens the door of her refrigerator, the refrigerator tells her spectacles that there is no more milk. Then, the refrigerator or the spectacles sends a tweet to her husband asking him to pick up a carton of milk on his way home. When her husband reaches home, his car talks to the garage door which automatically opens. Hurrying in to catch the cricket game on TV, he forgets to lock the car. When he switches on the TV, it notifies him about the unlocked car. Does it sound like a sci-fi movie to you? Wait for few years and it will become reality. In the next few years, you’ll most definitely see changes, which will dramatic impact your life. This is what we call“Internet of Things”.
